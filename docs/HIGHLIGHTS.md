# PingMeWhen — product highlights

How we talk about PingMeWhen, ranked. Lead with the emotional hook, then the
differentiator that makes it credible, then the proof. Do **not** sell "an AI
that makes calls" as the headline — that is table stakes now. The wedge is
control, restraint, and privacy; calling is just how it's delivered.

## 1. You stay in control — the AI knows when to stop *(hero)*

The AI handles the mechanical part and hands the conversation back the moment
judgment is needed. Every consequential action is yours to approve, and every
fact comes from you — so it never hallucinates a commitment or decides
something it shouldn't. In a moment when people are wary of agents acting on
their own, "the human is always the decision-maker" is both a safety story and
a quality story.

## 2. It gives you your attention back *(benefit)*

PingMeWhen does the tedious, repetitive, waiting part of getting something done
and pings you in only for the few seconds that actually need a person. You're
not stuck on the line — you get your time back. This is what the name promises.

## 3. Private by design *(moat)*

- Runs entirely on your machine, on your own keys — no hosted backend, no shared
  account, no shared credential store.
- Calls run over a secure tunnel with per-call, call-bound capability tokens that
  are revoked when the call ends and redacted from logs.
- Sensitive information (card number, CVV, SSN) never touches the AI model: the
  cloud model is muted, you type the value yourself, and it is spoken to the
  representative by on-device voice only — never sent to a model, never logged.

## 4. It talks to the real phone world *(scope)*

The places that matter — doctors, insurers, landlords, government offices, small
businesses — have no API, only a phone number. PingMeWhen reaches them over the
actual phone network, calling real people. Phone is the universal interface to
the analog world, and that's a real difference from API-only agents.

## 5. Fully observable, always yours to take back *(support / proof)*

Live transcript of both sides, listen in on the call audio in your browser, and
take over to speak (by typing) at any moment. You always know exactly what was
said on your behalf — it's never a black box.

---

**Do not claim** capabilities we haven't built: PingMeWhen does not navigate
automated phone menus / IVR trees, and does not connect live browser-microphone
audio. Say "waits and handles the back-and-forth," not "navigates phone menus."
